The TIME Alpe d'Huez X is not a gravel bike that compromises. It started as TIME's premier road racing platform — the same frame climbers carried up the actual Alpe d'Huez — then TIME opened up the tire clearance just enough to handle everything from fast pavement to packed gravel.

It does not feel like a road bike that got stretched. It feels like a road bike that decided to stop making excuses.

This build adds a 4iiii power meter on the GRX crank — a $350–400 training tool factory-installed, included in the price. The Hunt 45mm carbon wheels sit at the depth where road aerodynamics and gravel stability meet. Someone spec'd this with intention.

The frame is built using TIME's proprietary Braided Carbon Structure with Dyneema and Vectran fiber reinforcement — manufactured in France, not injection-moulded in bulk. The fibers run continuously through the structure without interruption.
